Action creates

In June 2020, we created the Peloton Pledge: a concrete action plan to combat systemic inequities. This plan addresses our internal culture and our principles, as well as a strategy for amplifying change both in our company and our community. It is an ambitious commitment that lays out our anti-racist vision with honesty, transparency and accountability.

"TOGETHER WE GO FAR" MEANS ALL OF US CAN’T WIN IF SOME OF US ARE BEING HELD BACK.

In this pivotal time in history, I believe we have an enormous responsibility to do our part to combat systemic racism, and I am committed to ensuring that we use our resources, platform and influence to change our society for the better—into a place where everyone can and will thrive.

John Foley, Peloton Co-Founder and CEO

We’re a Work in Progress

We’re not perfect, we know, but we are putting in the work. Through our growing DEI team, anti-racism conversation series and inclusion learning journeys, we are pushing ourselves to move toward meaningful and measurable change.

Building up the Ones Around Us

Strengthening Our Team From the Inside Out

We know everyone isn’t provided with the same opportunities to achieve their dreams. That’s why we’re making an effort to level the playing field. By investing $20 million into learning and development, we’re giving our Peloton team the education and resources to turn their aspirations into reality.

Investing in Wage Equality

Taking Care of Our Own

We commit to addressing global economic inequity, starting with investing $60 million to increase the hourly wages of our own workforce. After July 1, 2020, all North American- and European-based, non-commissioned hourly team members at Peloton now receive a starting wage of $19 an hour.
